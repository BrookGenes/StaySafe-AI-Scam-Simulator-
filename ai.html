AI Basics: Understanding Modern Scams

1) What Are AI Scams?

AI scams use computer technology to pretend to be someone you trust. Scammers use smart programs to copy faces, voices, and writing styles. Their goal is always the same: to trick you into giving money or information.

Key signs of an AI scam:

  • They pressure you to act fast.
  • They ask for money, gift cards, or banking info.
  • You feel confused or surprised by the message or call.

2) What Are Deepfakes?

A deepfake is a fake video or photo created using AI. It can make someone look like they said or did something they never did.

How to spot a deepfake:

  • The face may look too smooth or too perfect.
  • The voice might not match the mouth movements.
  • You may notice strange blinking or odd shadows.

3) What Is Voice Cloning?

Voice cloning uses AI to copy someone's voice. With just a short audio clip, scammers can create fake phone calls that sound real.

Examples of cloned voice scams:

  • A call pretending to be your grandchild asking for help.
  • A “bank manager” with a perfect, calm voice.
  • A fake emergency call asking for money right away.

How to protect yourself:

  • Always hang up and call the real person back.
  • Use a family “secret code word.”
  • Never send money without confirming through a trusted number.
